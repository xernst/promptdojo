I'm 22, I just graduated, and I think the job market is about to break for
anyone who can't work with AI.

That sounds dramatic. I don't think it is. Look at what already happened, not
what might. Copywriting teams that ran 40 people now run 6. Tier-one support got
hit first and hardest. Junior design, paralegal review, first-pass analysis: all
reshaped, most of it in the last two years. This isn't "AI is coming for tech
jobs." AI got cheap and fast at the structured, repeatable parts of almost every
job, and it happened everywhere at once.

Here's the part people get wrong. The skill that keeps you valuable isn't
"prompting" in the influencer sense. It's being able to point AI at real work,
read what it produced, and catch what it got wrong before it ships. That's a
real skill. It used to belong to programmers. It belongs to everyone now, and
almost nobody is being taught it.

So I built a school for it. It's called promptdojo, and it's free.

Let me be specific, because "free AI course" is a crowded and mostly bad
category. promptdojo teaches you to build by having you read code that AI
actually wrote, predict what it does, find the bug, and fix it. You do that 833
times, in your browser, with no installs. There are ten career paths, so a
marketer learns a different route than a lawyer or an operations manager. Nobody
gets handed a generic computer-science syllabus. The point isn't to make you a
software engineer. It's to make you the person who can direct AI instead of
being replaced by it.

It's free for individuals, and it's staying that way. No paywall, no "free
preview" that quietly runs out. It stays free because companies pay to train
their teams on it, and that funds the public version. I'd rather do it that way
than charge the people who are most exposed to all of this.

That's the real reason I built it. The next few years are going to be hard for a
lot of people whose work is changing faster than they can react to it. Putting a
paywall on the way out of that felt wrong, so I didn't.

If your job feels different than it did a year ago, this is for you. Go try it.
It takes about two minutes to start.

If you run a team that's going to have to reskill at scale, and most teams are,
this is for you too, and I'd genuinely like to talk. Reach out about training
your people on it.

And if it's not for you but you know someone it is for, send it to them. That's
the thing that actually helps.
